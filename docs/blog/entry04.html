Genome Contamination

2/20/2011

     This week, a PLOS One study on contamination of genomic data sets by human (researcher) data received a lot of press from the New York Times and other media.  Longo, et. al. screened 2749 non-primate data sets for repetitive elements that are only found in primate genomes, and found 492 to be contaminated with this sequence. 
     Although this study certainly illustrates the usefulness of skepticism in genomic data interpretation, this type of problem will not really have much of an effect on most studies that rely on this data.  In some cases, genome sequencing of less-well-studied organisms can be best said to push the envelope of our Biological knowledge, and it is better to view these genomes as rough first drafts.  It would seem that one take-home message from this publication is that it is best to be familiar with the genome version that you're working with, along with its possible faults. 
     However, I think it is also useful to view this type of problem in the broader context of the genome sequencing field.  Even though many research applications of next generation sequencing are highly tolerant of low-level contamination, de-novo human genome sequencing will really require extremely high standards if it is to be used for applications like disease allele screening or identification.  In contrast to PCR-based techniques that only screen specific loci in the genome, deep sequencing can potentially see any contaminant.  In the study mentioned here, this contamination was relatively easy to find, since we know that these repeats are only found in primates, but no similar strategy exists for human genome sequencing.
     This will become an increasingly relevant topic as we move toward therapeutic genome sequencing.  Such applications may require environmental standards that exceed our current crime lab standards to be really reliable.  Any center that is considering clinical application of sequencing methodologies should take this into account in the design and planning of such a facility.
